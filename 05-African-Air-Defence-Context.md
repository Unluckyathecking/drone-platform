# African Air Defence Context — Research Notes

## Why This Matters
Understanding the air defence landscape helps with:
- Risk assessment for drone operations
- Regulatory environment awareness
- Operational altitude planning
- Route planning around military zones

## Common Air Defence Systems in Sub-Saharan Africa

### MANPADS (Man-Portable Air-Defence Systems)
- **9K32 Strela-2 (SA-7 "Grail")** — very common
  - Range: ~3-4 km
  - Altitude: up to ~2-3 km
  - Guidance: infrared heat-seeking
- **9K38 Igla (SA-18)** — less common
  - Range: ~5 km
  - Altitude: up to ~3.5 km
  - Guidance: infrared heat-seeking

### Vehicle-Mounted Systems
- **FB-6A** — mobile SAM system
  - Uses FN-6 missiles (~5.5 km range)
  - Designed against helicopters, drones, low-flying aircraft

### Anti-Aircraft Guns
- **ZU-23-2** autocannons
- **ZPU** heavy machine-gun systems
- Often truck-mounted

## Implications for Drone Operations
- Small cargo drones (2-4 m wingspan) are very different targets from helicopters
- Low radar cross-section, low heat signature (electric motors)
- Operating with government approval eliminates military threat
- Key: work WITH authorities, not around them
- Regulatory approval is the real barrier, not air defence
