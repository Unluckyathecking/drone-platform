**Why they are worth approaching — but with caveats**

Tekever is the most operationally proven of the three. Their AR5 is live on the English Channel under a Home Office contract worth up to £1 billion. They are Europe's newest defence unicorn (valued at £1B+, May 2025), having announced a £400M UK investment programme. They are part of the RAF's Autonomous Collaborative Platforms programme. ## PART 3: NATO DIANA 2027 — ELIGIBILITY AND ACTION PLAN

### Eligibility Requirements (confirmed from DIANA official sources)

**Who can apply:**
- Any **incorporated company** with its principal place of business in a NATO member nation
- Must be **controlled and majority owned** by nationals of NATO member nations
- UK qualifies — the UK remains a NATO member and DIANA participant post-Brexit
- **SMEs and early-stage startups** are the primary target; DIANA explicitly states these will "benefit most" from the programme

**Who cannot apply:**
- Universities and non-profit organisations are **not eligible** — this is a hard rule
- Individuals applying personally (not through a company) are not eligible

**Key implication for Mohammed:**
A UK Ltd company must be registered before the application is submitted. Applying as an individual is not permitted. The company can have zero trading history and zero revenue — a newly incorporated Ltd is sufficient.

**TRL requirements:**
- Primary target: TRL 4–6
- "Lower TRL solutions with potential disruptive impact will be considered"
- The 2026 cohort included companies at TRL 3 with strong pitch narratives

**Application requirements (based on 2026 cycle):**
- One-page quad chart
- Maximum four-page technical proposal
- DUNS number (free, required before receiving funding — not required to submit)
- Description of: technology, team, market, challenge fit

**Funding:**
- Phase 1: €100,000 contractual funding (not grant — requires deliverables)
- Phase 2 (top performers): up to €300,000 additional
- Total possible: €400,000 (~£340,000)

### What Mohammed Must Do Before Applying to DIANA 2027

**Step 1 — Register a UK Ltd company (by June 2026)**

This is a hard requirement. A student cannot apply as an individual. Registering a Ltd company at Companies House costs £12 online and takes 24 hours.

Recommended company name options: MPE Systems Ltd / Cerulean C2 Ltd / Sensor Fusion Labs Ltd

Required:
- Company name
- Registered address (can use a virtual office address — approximately £10/month)
- At least one director (Mohammed, aged 17+, can be a director of a UK Ltd company — there is no minimum age for directors)
- Articles of association (use the standard model articles)

**Step 2 — Obtain a DUNS number (by June 2026)**

Free via dnb.com/duns-number. Takes 5–10 business days. Required before DIANA funding can be received (not before applying).

**Step 3 — Reach TRL 4 with documented evidence (by June 2026)**

TRL 4 definition: technology validated in a laboratory environment. For MPE, this means:
- Live ADS-B and AIS data demonstrated on a real internet connection (already done)
- Geofence enforcement demonstrated against a real track (already done in tests)
- CoT output verified against a TAK Server instance (FreeTAK Server, £0)
- Video demonstration of the system running (10-minute screen recording)

**Step 4 — Prepare the quad chart (by June 2026)**

One A4 page with four quadrants:
1. Problem statement: multi-domain track fusion gap for GNSS-denied UAS
2. Technology: MPE architecture diagram
3. Team: Mohammed's profile, A-level results, GitHub stats
4. Market: 150 countries that cannot access ITAR-controlled C2 systems; £500K–£5M market per deployment

**Step 5 — Choose the right challenge area**

Top two options for 2027 (based on 2026 challenge structure):
1. **Autonomy and Unmanned Systems** — direct fit for MPE as the C2 layer for autonomous drone fleets
2. **Data Assisted Decision Making** — fits the classifier, pattern-of-life, and SITREP generation capabilities

Apply to whichever challenge area is most actively seeking solutions in the 2027 call. Both are plausible. Autonomy + UAS is more defensible given the live code.

**Step 6 — Contact Janus Allies in advance**

Email janus@iottribe.org in May 2026 (before the call opens) to introduce the project and ask for pre-application guidance. This is explicitly encouraged by DIANA. It also creates a relationship with the UK accelerator team who will evaluate the application.
