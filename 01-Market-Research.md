# Market Research — Cargo Drone Delivery in Africa

## The Opportunity

Rural Africa has massive unmet logistics needs — poor roads, vast distances, urgent medical supply chains. Companies like Zipline have proven the model works. The opportunity is building **ultra-efficient, lightweight fixed-wing cargo drones** for last-mile delivery.

## Existing Players

### Zipline (Rwanda, Ghana)
- Fixed-wing catapult-launched drones
- Delivers blood, vaccines, medicines, emergency supplies
- ~190 km range on small electric motors
- Payload dropped by parachute (no landing required)
- Drone recovered by capture wire system
- Platform 1: proven at scale

## Target Use Cases (Legal & High-Demand)
- Rural medical delivery (blood, vaccines, lab samples)
- Island logistics
- Disaster relief
- Mining site supply runs
- Offshore platform supply
- Agricultural supply delivery

## Countries With Strong Demand
- **Rwanda** — already has Zipline infrastructure, receptive to drone logistics
- **Ghana** — active drone delivery operations
- **Sierra Leone** — limited road infrastructure, diamond mining regions
- **Central African Republic** — remote mining areas, minimal infrastructure
- **Liberia** — poor roads, gold/diamond regions

## Why Road-Based Logistics Fails in These Regions
- Unpaved/seasonal roads
- Rainy seasons make roads impassable
- Long distances between population centres
- Checkpoints add delays (DRC, Sierra Leone, CAR)
- Security concerns on remote roads

## Competitive Advantage to Target
- Lower cost per unit than Zipline
- Simpler launch/recovery (no catapult/wire needed?)
- Open-source autopilot (PX4/ArduPilot) to reduce costs
- Designed for local manufacturing/maintenance
